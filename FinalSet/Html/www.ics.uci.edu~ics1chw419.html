Introduction

Censorship of any kind is wrong and the internet is not an exception. The Bill of Rights says that congress shall amend no law that takes away a right from the people specifically stated in the constitution. For the government to censor people who use the internet is a direct violation of the Bill of Rights, as is reading someone's electronic mail(e-mail). We should have a right to do whatever we want as long as it does not inflict harm on any one else. Additionally, what is in someone's e-mail directory is their business and no one else's. The government needs to understand that the Bill of Rights cannot be ignored where the internet is concerned.

Issues

The internet has a variety of resources located within its realms. There are things like Gopher and the World Wide Web, which are divisions of the Internet that contain even more information. The Internet also contains Internet Relay Chat(IRC), which allows people from all over the world to talk to each other. There is a lot of information out there and for the government to try to censor some of it is wrong. That is limiting some of the resources and, when we start limiting our resources, we are possibly taking away some valuable information. Even though the government may not feel that the information is valuable, it can still have value if other people feel it does.

There is also the issue of privacy, because, in order for Congress to censor the internet, they will have to have access to everyone's account. This is not censorship but rather and invasion of privacy. What I keep in my account is my business. Unless there is some way that what I have in my account can hurt someone, then the government has no business reading what is in it. There is the problem of how the government could find out if there is something harmful in someone's account. This issue of privacy recently came up in a case involving a guy who detailed the brutal rape and murder of a girl in one of his classes. He used the girls name and detailed the story on IRC. Because of this incident, the government wants to have access to all e-mail accounts. That would not be legal under current jurisdiction, because, in June, 1967, a joint committee, comprised of representatives from the American Association of University Professors, U. S. National Student Association, Association of American Colleges, National Association of Student Personnel Administrators, and National Association of Woman Deans and Counselors, met in Washington, D.C., and drafted the Joint Statement on Rights and Freedoms of Students(JSRF) The Joint Statement on Rights and Freedoms of Students proves that even if searches by the government were allowed, they could not possibly be very effective if they were done legally. In Article VI., section B. Investigation of Student Conduct, part 1. of the JSRF, it is clearly stated that " Except under extreme emergency circumstances, premises occupied by students and the personal possessions of students should not be searched unless appropriate authorization has been obtained. Only in extreme cases could searches be done, therefore, there would be very few cases where the government would be obliged to access someone's account. Someone's e-mail account is there personal possession and should fall under the rules and guidelines of the Joint Statement of Rights and Students.

Sites

An example of a site that might be considered for censorship is the March 1995 Playmate page. It contains a picture of Stacey Sanchez. If this were just a normal picture, then there probably would not be any problem. This however is not a normal picture. Stacey Sanchez is the March 1995 Playboy Playmate of the Month and the picture is a nude photograph of Ms. her. I understand that many people may consider this pornography and therefore, want to ban the picture and/or the whole Playboy site itself. There is nothing wrong the naked human body and therefore, nothing wrong with having access to a picture of the naked human body. I understand that this is a personal view, but to say that the picture is pornographic and should be banned is also exercising personal opinion. The only difference is that the government is imposing its personal opinion on others. If the government bans the picture and others like it, such as these nude pictures of Elisa Bridges and Lisa Marie Scott, it is not giving those who like these kinds of pictures a choice of whether or not they can see the pictures. Whereas, if the government were to leave the picture site alone, then everyone would have a choice. People could choose to either look at the picture or not. No one is stopping them from doing either. Also, if the majority of people feel that children should not be allowed to look at the pictures, there are steps that can be taken to prevent their access. For instance, at the First Amendment Privacy Curtain for Ambers Playhouse(FAPCAP), the user is asked to enter their age. This is the equivalent of 900 numbers saying that children under the age of eighteen must get parent permission before calling or that you must be at least eighteen years of age to call. Of course, someone under twenty-one could always type in that they are twenty-one or older, just as someone under the age of eighteen could call a 900 number without their parent's permission. To stop unauthorized people from calling 900 numbers from their number, Pacific Bell allows people to put a 900 number block on their phone. There is no such "block" available on the internet as yet that I have been able to locate, however little kids are not the ones buying the computers in most cases and they are also not the ones signing up for e-mail accounts, either. Parents are the ones who control the use of the computers in their home. If they do not want their kids to have access to material they deem inappropriate, then there are a few alternatives they have. The parents can supervise their children when they use the computer, they cannot let their kids know the password to their e-mail accounts, or they could lock the keyboard, since almost all computers have a lock that can be engaged when desired.

Benefits

The internet offers many benefits to its users. Each of the various sights on the internet or the World Wide Web or Gopher has something to offer. Additionally the sites that someone finds will have links to other sites and that site will have links and so on. The information is endless and each day, more information is added. I am a rap music fan and "Vibe" is a rap magazine that I like to read. On the World Wide Web, there is the Vibe Magazine Homepage. It has articles on rappers and even has clips of music videos. This is only one of the many sites on the internet.

Agencies

There are many agencies out there that attempt to prevent censorship and protect people's right to privacy, especially on the Internet. One organization is the Electronic Frontiers Foundation of New Hampshire, EFF. The EFF attempts "to ensure protection of freedom of speech and privacy in electronic communication; to help policy-makers, the media, and the general public understand the online world; and to help the citizens of New Hampshire gain access to government information electronically." Organizations like the EFF try to watch out for ordinary internet users who otherwise would not know that the government is trying to take away some of their rights. Without such organizations, we might not have some of the rights we currently enjoy.

Conclusion

What is located in someone's e-mail file is their personal business. It is as personal as a letter that is written. The government does not have the right to read anyone's mail unless it an extreme circumstance and the same should hold true for an e-mail account. Until Congress can prove that it is possible for someone to be physically injured by something that they see or hear while using the internet, then I do not see just cause for censorship of the internet. Too many people in this world rely on the internet for the government to censor some of the sites or some of the pictures located on the internet. There are various avenues that can be taken to prevent the unauthorized use of the internet by those people who are underage for that to be an excuse. The benefits of the internet so far outway any minute costs that there is no need for the government to attempt to censor it.
