A Road to the Stars; Space Research Vehicles

  • Somewhere in the vast reaches of the universe lies a galaxy. Within that galaxy the planet Earth travels in an endless rotation around the planet in the center of its Solar System the Sun. On this planet is a lifeform like no other. These life forms are known as human beings. For thousands of years these humans have been fascinated with the Heavens, and even entertained notions of space travel. The last part of this century has seen these fascinations become reality. However only in the last three decades has it been possible for man to place his tools and himself beyond Earth's atmosphere. Americas spacecraft have taken men to the Moon, landed on Mars, and explored the region around Jupiter's moons. They have also mapped Earth, and provide continousmeterological data from Hawaii to Maine, and from Alaska to Florida.

  • American spacecraft programs are separated into three categories. NASA managed programs which are primarily scientific in nature. Which include spacecraft such as the Viking, Voyager, Magellen, Galileo and Mariner 10 satelites used to probe Earth as well as other planets. NASA has also managed unmanned landings on the Moon, Venus, and Mars as well as the many manned trips to the Moon in theApollo lunar landings.
  • The second type of program is that of commercial spacecraft. Which for the most part are designed and built by private manufacturers. For example the RCA company built and launched it's own satelite system called SATCOM. Another large company that takes advantage of space age technology is AT&T. They use the HS376 satelite made by the Hughes aircraft company. This particular satelite is a good example of a succesful commercial aircraft. Hughes has built and marketed 30 of theses aircraft to companies located around the globe.
  • The third type of spacecraft are military spacecraft, like NASA many of the militaries aircraft have been built by private customers. There has always been intense competition between the United States military services, but that has been replaced by cooperation between forces. The Air Force conducts the majority of Department of Defense launches including those of Navy Spacecraft. The Air Force manages the NAVSTAR Global Positioning satelite, but all of the armed forces use it.

  • American spacecraft are launched from three sites within the United States. These are the Kennedy Space Center at Cape Canaveral, Florida. Wallops Flight Facility at Wallops Island, Virginia. The last would be Vandenberg Air Force Base in California. Kennedy Space Center is responsible for the launches of all the planetary probes, and also all of the manned space flights.

  • Officially known as the Space Transportation System, the space shuttle is the most important earth-orbit program ever undertaken by the United States. The space shuttle can be used to perform scientific experiments, carry satelites and place them into orbit, it can be used to retrieve satelites and repair them in space. The shuttle can carry both civilian and military payloads. The basic idea of having a space shuttle solely rests on the concept of a reusable spacecraft. The shuttle orbiter is basically the largest spacecraft ever put into orbit, It posed no small problem for designers. It would have to be launched like a rocket, orbit like a spacecraft and return to Earth like an airplane. The designers final result was the space shuttle. There were six space shuttles built in all, The Enterprise, Discovery, Endeavor, Atlantis, Columbia and Challenger
