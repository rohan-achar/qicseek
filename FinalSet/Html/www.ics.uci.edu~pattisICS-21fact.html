Course Help

Instructor: Please feel free to drop by any time during my office hours, without an appointment: mine are open office hours. Also, I frequently attend lab, which is another time that students can ask me quesions, including questions about the lab and general questions about the course. If you cannot make any of these times and want to schedule an appointment at some other time, call or e-mail me to arrange one (or, if the question is small, just send me e-mail asking it). I answer e-mail periodically throughout the day (from about 8:30am to 10:00pm). I will also hold online office hours (Monday-Thursday and Sunday, 9:00pm to 10:00pm) where I am reachable by by AIM handle richardepattis.

Staff: The TAs and Lab Tutors are avaiable during labs to help students. Don't hesitate to approach them with questions. I encourage students to talk about programming with the staff and with each other: asking and answering questions, and understanding the answers to their questions (and if not, asking more questions). Just as with understanding a natural language, the more you talk about programming (and program) the easier it will become.

Online: The most useful online mechanism for handing questions/discussions is EEE's Forums on MessageBoard These forums allow students to ask questions (or start discussions) easily, as well as supply answers (or participate in the discsussion further). Initially, I have created four forums for ICS-21:

  1. Java and Eclipse
  2. Lecture Material
  3. Programming Assignments
  4. Quizzes and Exams
If the need arises for other forums (tell me if you think another would be useful), I will create them. You can instruct EEE to email you links to any active discussions in the forums. In past quarters, these forums have seen a tremendous amount of traffic: course material has been clarified, questions have been asked and answered, and many interesting side-discussions have ensued. But the efficacy depends on your participation.

I will broadcast information to all the students (and staff) in ICS-21 using the

    ICS-21 Email List (ics21-S12@classes.uci.edu)
All students and staff in the course will automatically receive emails sent to this address and all messages sent via this discussion list will be archived on the Email Archive web page. Mostly student-initiated questions/discussions should use the forums mentioned above, but sometimes students can use this email list to inform the instructor, staff, and other students about some timely issue: e.g., Checkmate not responding to program submissions.

Finally remember to read the Announcements link daily, on the course web; I tend to post long-lived information here, rather than via email.

It is well known (but underappreciated) fact that consistent attendence of lectures/lab is strongly correlated with good course performance; therefore, I expect all my students to attend classes regularly. Plan on arriving punctually -I make important announcements at the start of each class- paying attention, and treating your classmates with respect.

If you do miss a class, your first point of contact should be other students who attended the class (or the TA for your section, but not me) to determine what you missed and how you can catch up (see the course Web too).

Bottom Line

As a lecturer at UCI, my primary responsiblity is teaching; my primary activities are preparing course materials, teaching in class, interacting with students (in class, lab, and during office hours) and grading. I plan to attend all lectures and labs -you should too.
