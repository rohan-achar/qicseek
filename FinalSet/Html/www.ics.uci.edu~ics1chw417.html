COMPUTER USAGE IN THE CLASSROOM ENVIRONMENT

Computers: the new teachers aid.

Written by Sandra Sosa easu1577 and Wendy Cheng easu517

Computers are growing rapidly and stronger every day. Just about a decade ago, computers existed but were not widely implemented in the classroom. Today, however, children in the first grade are very aware of computers and are computer literate. This is the computer generation. Kids in malls are now playing with the computers displayed at Sears or Radio Shack with great ease. Now computers are taking a new step by taking an active part in education. In reality, it is not so new since pilot projects have been out there for a number of years, but now almost every school has at least one computer on their campus.

Computers are now being tied to education and are used as tools to benefit the learning process. In fact computers have revolutionized education by bringing in new teaching methods that are current with the rapid technological changes. We are begining to explore what we can call the tip of the iceberg when it comes to the potential computers and the internet has in aiding the classroom environment. Science and math have been the focus of many internet projects and lesson plans for teachers are available on line thru the internet. Many agencies have taken an interest on how the internet can be use and are exploring the posibilities of appling the use of the net to every subject taught in school. For example Nasa, and the Smithsonian Institution have created their home page with a list of sources related to education and the uses of the internet.

Teachers are no longer working in a classroom alone. Through the network, they are able to exchange lesson plans, communicate with other educators, and compare methodology. We are now hitting a new era of Educational Reform and Computers. In fact, the University of Houston has established a teacher education internet server. This server provides access to an internet gopher ( these are documents stored int he net which may contain sound and images) and discussion groups (these are interactive discussions in the Internet). Teachers here can be informed of the latest developments.

With the teachers being well-informed, they now have to transmit this to their classroom. The Department of Education at UCI has worked with a project called KIDS-NET. It is a forerunner of the concept of the usage of the Internet in education. It was funded by National Geographic, and the Drug Free Schools of the Department of Education conducted these studies. They had their own central site that was assisted by Sprint Net. Schools subscribe to this privately owned net and would get a unit to work with. The introductory unit was called HELLO, and they were hooked up to other schools. Students would connect on to this net and have interaction with other schools. Several schools in the United States were connected through this net during the summer. A student from California could compare the rainfall here with a student all the way in Texas. It was aimed at children in the elementary level and focused on having students do hands-on work in the sciences. UCI was in charge of administering the pre and post testing and analyzing the statistical data to ensure that the project was working properly.

There is now another project out there that schools are using per semester called the Computer as Learning Partner (CLP). It is an integrated energy curriculum teaching the physical sciences topics of heat, light and sound at the middle school level. Schools are now aiming at a hands-on, laboratory-based curriculum for almost all subjects rather than just a text-based curriculum. Schools want more student-teacher interaction, and that is what the CLP project has to offer. It is not serving as a substitute for the teacher, but acting as a backbone to the class. Computers provide the tools that textbooks cannot give to th e students. It emphasizes realistic problems. Computers act as partners to the students by providing the components to perform experiments and sample models.

New teaching strategies are being developed to complement the use of computers. Many fear that by having children sit in frot of a computer to learn may isolate them into a world of their own. But teaching strategies such as cooperative learning and new sources of assessment along with computer usage complement each other. Cooperative learning involves student intearction in the classroom. The teacher is not the only one talking and teaching the lesson. Students are grouped to work together emphasizing their strong points. This could be done in every subject. Teachers are also trying to integrate the subject and not separating math with science and english but making on flow with the other. Computers can do since it is able to integrate more than one topic. Finaly the use of new assessment methods such as using a portfolio and teaching other children make a difference in learning. Test can not always tell a teacher if in fact the student is learning. Computers help the retention of a child since they see it, read it, explore it, interact with it, and enjoy it.

Educational use of computers is not limited to the classroom setting, but it could also be integrated in the home. Science at home was piloted in Spring of 1994, and was presented to families to see how each family member can participate in scientific discovery. Computer usage in education has been quite successful, and will soon become an essential part of the classroom setting.
