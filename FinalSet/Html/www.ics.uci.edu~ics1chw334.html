  1. The World Wide What?!?

    The World Wide Web was begun only last decade at CERN, the European Laboratory for Particle Physics in Switzerland. WWW is a universe of ideas and information accessed via network and is a research tool of immense magnitude. Though the Web is not dependant on the Internet to function, it is dependant on it to access the volumes of information that is out there. The Web was conceived as a means to share information without regards to the various different protocols and emulations that computers use. HTML is the backbone of the Web. Hypertext markup language allows one to jump (almost) instantaneously from one document to another. A web browser is necessarry to use html. NSCA Mosaic is the web browser of choice at the moment. However, there are many other browsers out there such as Lynx, NetScape, Cello and many others. They all offer basic web access while some offer multimedia capabilities.

  2. HTML? What possible use can it be to me?

    Spending hours upon hours online cruising the web can be a very social thing. In fact there is a whole culture known as geeks that does just that. My favorite bunch is the Santa Cruz geeks. For a peek at what they're up to, check out the SCAM or Santa Cruz Alternative Music page. This page also offers links to geek happenings. Html is their voice to the world. Many geeks have their own pages and commune in geek houses which often house web servers of their own.

    But html isn't just about being social. As a research tool, the Web is absolutely amazing. You can visit the Icole Normale Supirieure, a student server in France, any major university (and even some smaller ones) in the world, a restaurant guide for your favorite city and even shop electronically. And, of course, with a decent knowledge of html you can post web pages of your own.

  3. To be or not to be...at the Rennaissance Faire?

    So what is this Faire thing anyway? Throughout the year there are recreations of the medieval and rennaissance time periods of Europe held all over the country. In California the major events are the Rennaissance Pleasure Faires, northern and southern , run by the Living History Center. These particular faires feature authentic culture of the Elizabethan (Shakespearean) period: food, entertainment, and even a battle or two. Keep in mind that it is always more enjoyable to go to faire in period costume . The actors treat you much better and you'll get a better feel for rennaissance life if you can join in. The SCA (Society for Creative Anachronism) is dedicated to recreating and chronicalling the time period and also offers wonderful insight to medieval and rennaissance life.

  4. How would one say, "You have dishonored my hamster?" in Klingon?

    "nay'qoqvammo' DaHoHta" means (if I have interpreted my research properly) "You have dishonored my tribble." In the Klingon language "tribble" is the closest approximation to hamster. After all, isn't a tribble just a hamster with no legs?

    Didn't you ever wish you cold toss around a few phrases in a foreign language? Spanish and French just a little too boring for you? Then try the Klingon Language Institute . Here you can look up any Klingon phrase in the archives, research Klingon history, see the writing system, and even become a fluent member of the Institute.

  5. Momma, I hungry. Whats for dinner? Where to find gourmet food.

    One of my favorite snacks is gourmet popcorn. Yummy, yummy. I don't care what the media says about it being healthy, unhealthy or otherwise. Meyers Gourmet Popcorn will deliver your order right to your door and even gift wrap it in a nifty, pretty tin.

    But one can not live by popcorn alone. So for the true gourmand I offer you the eGG , or electronic Gourmet Guide, an e-zine absolutely devoted to food and cooking. Right now they're only on issue #5 and it's already an awesome little zine. They have interviews with famous chefs, quick tips and piles of recipes as well as reviews of cook books. This is one of my favorite pages because, in my house, if I want anything besides chinese food to eat, I have to make it myself.

    Bon apetit!
