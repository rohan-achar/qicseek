Human Vision

Human visual field: about 200 degrees of arc  
Fovea(area of highest resolution): about 2 degrees of arc  
    75% of the human visual operations are related to the fovea.
    Human vision is not like a camera; does not take a complete picture at once

Light reception takes place in the retina, which contains 2 types of photoreceptors:

Three types of cones:

  • red-sensitive: 64%, high concentration in fovea area
  • green-sensitive: 32%, high concentration in fovea area
  • blue-sensitive: 2%, evenly distributed over retina; none in the center of the fovea.
