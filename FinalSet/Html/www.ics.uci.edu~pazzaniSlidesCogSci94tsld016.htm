PostHoc Control Structure

  • Productions examine

    • Current hypothesis
    • Current example
    • Background knowledge

  • Propose new hypotheses (if current example misclassified)

      1. Initialize hypothesis

      2. Error of omission (positive example classified as negative)
      3. Error of commission (negative example classified as positive)

  • Each production set is ordered: Productions that make use of background knowledge have higher priority than productions that do not use background knowledge .

  • Combines inductive and analytical learning: Inductive productions may refine initial analytical hypothesis
