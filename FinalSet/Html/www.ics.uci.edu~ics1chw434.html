Tribal Sovereignty

by Elizabeth Whipple - eafu184

The indifferent relationship that exists with the United States government to American Indians tribes across the nation has been unresolved since the days of Columbus. Many tribes hold the government accountable for the numerous broken treaties, and have yet to be given a response. The issue of tribal sovereignty will remain at the forefront for the Indian people and will not just go away.

Sovereignty is the right to be autonomous and have self-determination. For an entire ethnicity of people to be stripped of this right, even though they practiced self-governance before the Europeans and the Spanish people came to the area which is now known as North America, it is only right that these people should want to regain their fundamental right to maintain a distinct community.

Through tribal sovereignty, American Indians hold a unique status as citizens. Not only are they subject to laws and procedures of the United States government, but to tribal governments as well. An example of this is that most Americans have a Social Security number, which is a universal identifier; whereas an Indian person will not only have a Social Security number, but have a Census or Tribal Verification number which identifies that person as a member of a federally recognized tribe within the United States.

By means of a treaty, the U.S. government took the first steps in establishing a contingent relationship with American Indian tribes. The first of such agreements took place during the period of the Revolutionary War with the group of tribes known as the Six Nations of the Iroquois Confederacy, which include the

  • Mohawks
  • Oneidas
  • Onondagas
  • Tuscaroras
  • Cayugas and
  • Senecas.
Many of the treaties first stipulated boundary lines of what the government had control over, also the alliance between the two agreeing parties would prevent the tribes from attacking non-Indians or they would face extreme punishments. An example of the before mentioned agreement is the sovereignty within the United States was the Oneida nation. They have continued to honor their side of the treaty and have remained an ally to the United States.

Previously known as the War Department,the Department of the Interior plays an integral role in that it is responsible for public lands that American Indians reside on. An important aspect to having tribal sovereignty is having a land base where self-governance can occur. Throughout history, the government has designated the location and size of the land areas through treaties. Along the way, the agreements were adjusted or overlooked in various situations to benefit governmental and non-Indian purposes. Such as:

  1. settlers wanting to cross Indian lands to reach untouched areas,
  2. to establish settlements for farming, and
  3. to have access to valuable resources located on tribal lands.
These broken promises are the means by which tribal Indians have rallied together and have asked the United States government to honor these treaties.

The Indian people have found that by educating themselves in regards to their own tribal history, they are then better prepared to enter into discussions with the government. Individuals who are well-informed usually become advocates for their tribes and can succinctly retrace the history of broken promises.

After five hundred years, the United States government still has not seen fit to honor treaties. However; under the current office of President Bill Clinton, some remarkable steps have taken place in reaffirming past treaties between the U.S. government and Indian nations. In April of 1994, President Clinton invited the tribal leaders representing over five hundred tribes throughout the United States to Washington D.C. and gave an address "stating that federally recognized tribes have the sovereign authority to interact on a government-to-government basis directly with federal agencies."

It is unfortunate to realize that after hundreds of years the Indian people still have to make a request to the United States government for the right to self-determination. It is interesting to ponder how many more years it will take to be granted that request with no stipulations attached.
