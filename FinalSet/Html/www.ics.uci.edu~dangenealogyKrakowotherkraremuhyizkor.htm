Remuh Shul Yahrzeits: circa ~1939

The Remuh Synagogue yahrzeits are hand calligraphed in Hebrew on a series of wall plaques (one per month) made of some kind of tough cardboard or canvas. They were hung by strings on hooks in the synagogue.

Although the Remuh Synagogue is still in operation, these lists were collected for safekeeping by the Central Jewish Historical Commission in the years following World War II, and are now held by the Archives of the Jewish Historical Institute of Poland, bearing File No. 1011 within the Krakow Jewish Community collection.

The list below covers the yartzeit of 311 individuals who died during the years 1886 to 1939. Most of the entries have full details including surname, first name, name of parent and year and date of death. The corresponding Gregorian year has been added in the last column. Illegible names have been left blank.

(Data provided by Alex Kerner. Thanks to Yale Reisner and staff of the Jewish Historical Museum in Warsaw, and the Ronald S. Lauder Foundation)
