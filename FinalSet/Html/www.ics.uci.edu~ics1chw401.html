Introduction:

This article is intended to help Educators, Parents and Children better utilize their Web resources. Included in this paper is information pertaining to the TEACHER who is interested in better exploring the Web and the idea of using it within the classroom as an additional teaching tool. Also included are sites throughout the Web that PARENTS can utilize at home to better educate themselves on how they can interact with their children and the education process. Also given are sites that would be fun to peruse with children. A section on what KIDS can do on the Web has also been included. Everything from sports information to actual games that can be played. The goal is to create a resource that can be used by educators, parents and children.

Teachers

Teachers, where do you begin? Many educators have yet to explore the world of Cyberspace. This world of Cyberspace can seem confusing. Many questions and doubts may arise to a teacher who is considering using a computer as another teaching tool within the classroom:
  1. What is the history of the internet?
  2. What are some internetinteractive classroom activities that involve the computer?
These questions and more will be explored in the next few pages.

History of the Internet
The Internet or information highway was born in 1969 although it had been thought over theoretically for over 25 years. It was instigated by the U.S. Department of Defense as a way for computers at distant locations to "talk" with each other. This communication was to be used to transfer military and national security data. Well, today it has developed into much more. It is now a source that can be used by people all over the world to transfer data and exchange information.

Educational Resources for Educators
There are some good resources on the Web that a beginner may access. There is an educational Web server called Web 66 that contains among it's many educational goals, the goal of helping educators of grades K-12 learn how to set up their own Internet servers. The Web 66 Classroom Internet Server Cookbookmay be a helpful tool for the beginner Webber. It contains information on adding pictures and sound to the page that you create. It also includes information on how to mail share and creating your own mailing list. In it's Server Kitchen you are able to find all the basic information on beginning an educational connection to the Web. Also helpful is the Home Page of Macintosh, you can learn all about their various servers. There are also links to other home pages where you can find out more about different types of computers. Please note, however, that not all of your technical problems and questions can be found answered on the Web for you. Many technical questions may need to be addressed to someone that you can talk to directly.

There are many resources on the Web that are available for teachers. One such source that was previously mentioned was Web 66. Web not only contains informative computer server information, but it also contains links to educational documents all over the Web. Web 66 includes a What's New page and even a registry of all the K-12 schools on the Web. This can be useful because a teacher can look into how other classrooms and schools are using the Web. Another example of Web resources is Janice's OUTPOST. Her K-12 Cyberspace OUTPOST contains group of very helpful educational virtual libraries on the Web. One interesting and informative link is called the AskERIC Virtual Library. This Library contains everything from a complete 6 week lesson plan outline on Astrologyto great listed resources on mainstreaming gifted students.

An informative source for teachers would be the FLTeach page. This page supplies different information on foreign language teaching methods for high school and college educators. A very informative site and good starting point for a foreign language educator would be the World Wide Web page of Foreign Language Resources. This site includes links all over the United States and the world. It contains information on many different foreign languages, from Hebrew to Chinese.

One interesting and informative Web page is that of the Cyberspace Middle School. On this page you can find helpful information on Web magazines and a special search engine on the web just for educators, called CEARCH in the Cisco Educational Archive.. Now if you are looking for state of the art development for education through computers the Web has information available on that, also. One study called Computer as Learning Partner Project(CLP) is actually founded by the National Science Foundation. This project, directed by Dr. Marcia Linn of Berkeley's Graduate School of Education. attempts to help students with the conceptual complexities of understanding science. This project uses the computer to assist in instruction. CLP has become a complete physical science course being offered at Foothill Middle School in Walnut Creek, CA. RAND (Research and Development) is a non-profit institute that that has seen the need for computer teaching tools. A system by the name of ARC/INFO ESSCOTS created with the help of the RAND corporation, allows junior high and high school students to propose statistical theories and then examine them with easy-to-use graphical interfaces.

Another project allows high school students to exchange text, data, audio and video with other users. This is the Learning through Collaborative Visualization Project(CoVis) supported by Ameritech and Bellcore companies. This educational network uses a standard of computing usually only accesible to commercial networking.

Interactive Classroom Activities

Nothing can completely replace the hands-on traditional experiences that a classroom and teacher are able to provide. With the computer and the communication attributes thereof, one can communicate with other classrooms throughout the state and throughout the world.

Teachers are now able to have contact with other teachers and exchange innovative educational ideas, while students are capable of communicating with other students all over the world. By this communication they can peer edit assignments or cultivate friendships. The Internet gives the modern educator a new classroom tool. This tool can become an educational window to other cultures around the world. For example at John H. Springman School a teacher or school administrator can explore the teaching and motivational philosophy of another school without actually visiting the campus. Students can talk to other students at the school and teachers can also communicate and exchange ideas. On Steve Stratford's home page you and your students can look at schools and their computer projects from around the world. One can even look at a classroom in Germany, view their class picture and be accessible to each students very own home page. St. Olaf College in Minnesota helps out with the Intercultural E-Mail Classroom Connections program. A teacher can link themselves and their classes to other classes in different countries, thereby exposing their classes to other cultures. One interesting site includes an interactive on-line discussion on AfroAmerican migration form the Southern States of the U.S., to Chicago from 1942 through 1970. Interactive sites such as this one would greatly augment a unit on AfroAmerican History. Another site would tie in well with a unit on war. This page includes historical war stories along with professional exhibits and pictures and memories from veterans.

Educational Uses for Parents
Parents as well as teachers are able to use the Internet to educate their children. The Internet can accommodate all ages of children in a variety of different subjects. A good place for parents to begin looking would be in the Kids Web which is a World Wide Web Digital Library for Schoolkids. In this directory one can choose from a multiplicity of subjects to engage their children.

This particular Kids Web has many subject headings in the area of art. As well there are a vast majority of sciences that have to do with the environment, physics, weather, biology and the educational list continues. This Kids Web also has directories pertaining to Social Studies and Miscellaneous themes. Each one of these documents link to other computers and documents that provides a vast array of incredible information to teach children. This is why the Internet is called a web, the resource of knowledge is never ending.

Simple Exploration

A good way to start is just simple exploration. For example under the arts, (ArtsEdge) there are amazing pictures. A child is able to take a tour of the famous Louvre art gallery in Paris just by sitting down at their computer. Dramas can also be shown on the screen (although this could take a long time to load onto one's computer depending on the type of computer and it's speed). The subject of sciences can take a child through museums and show them pictures of dinosaurs. Pictures of dinosaurs are only a few of the great online images available to see, as well there are many types of birds, reptiles, amphibians, insects or mammals to find. In the area of social studies, children are able to look into information about our government and the different branches that exist in our judical system. One's interest is continuously spurred when exploring the Internet. The Internet is a learning experience for both the parent and the child.

The Internet also allows for parents to look into the methodology that other model school districts use. From on-line examples, parents can get a good feel for how others are choosing to educate their children. A nice example of this would be technology Mission Statement, about their newspaper, board policies, manuals and a basic overall general Netscape which is able to show graphics, Sparkle which is able to show movies online, SoundMachine and many others. (Sparkle and SoundMachine can only be accessed with appropriate equipment.) All these extras are fun to have and the functions they contribute are amazing to watch on the computer because the computer just seems to come to life.

Educational Uses for Children

Computers are part of the future and children need to be familiar with computers in this day and age. Whether or not a child is playing games or finding new and exciting web find, they are familiarizing themselves with the computer and it's many functions.

Places for Kids to Start

A fantastic directory with endless topics and links for children is Uncle Bob's Kids' Page. "This page is a treasure chest of links, which have been cleaned, checked, and annotated, with spotlights on special subject." This is the introduction to Uncle Bob's Page that Bob Allison (boba@wwa.com) created. Within this directory there are spotlights on outer space, the grand canyon national park, Star Trek, pictures, upcoming, current and recent events, Disney, NASA and much much more because these highlights don't even scratch the surface of what there is to find.

There are many games to find in Uncle Bob's Page, but there are also many other places to look. Fun and Games in another particular directory contains comics and many different varieties. Games also seem to be unlimited on the Internet. There is everything from Backgammon, Paintballs, Role Playing Games, Scrabble to Television Game Shows. The lists are numerous and on this homepage there are the options of hobbies a$ zines/">on-line magazines. Kids can be glued to the screen for hours once they find this directory.

Sports

Another area that kids usually enjoy is the area of sports. The Yahoo Sports Page is a fantastic place to start. There is about 100 different sports that can be looked into. Any of these sports will lead to a homepage where more can be researched or current news can be looked up. It contains sports from croquet, korfball, sumo, martial arts to snow boarding. It appears to be the "most comprehensive listing of sports information available on the Internet." (Paul Coddington, paulc@npac.syr.edu)

KID which stands for "Kids Internet Delight" is one more site that children might really enjoy. This directory is basically an index of an array of different information for children to search. Learning can be fun on a computer but there are also many areas that children can explore that may not be something that parent feel suitable for their children to view. With young children it is recommended that parents monitor and help their child while exploring the web. Searching the web is definitely an educational experience for children regardless of their age and the familiarity with a computer will be helpful to them in the future.

Conclusion

Exploring the Internet leads to many educational finds. Teachers, Parents and Children are each able to resource particular areas to accommodate their needs. Teachers can use the web for informational ideas in helping to more effectively teach their children. Parents are able to get involved with their children's learning process by actively finding interesting topics or pictures. Children are able to explore a wide range of exciting themes, games, sports and much more. The Internet is growing daily and has so much knowledge to offer the world that it is important to get involved. The Internet will be a leading source of knowledge in the future and it is important to get children involved. Teachers and Parents have the opportunity to be a part of it and educate the future generations.
