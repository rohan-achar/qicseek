Introduction -- Singapore , the Paradise Island

Singapore is a very small tropical island occupying only 622.6 square kilometers of land. It is located at the Southern tip of the Malaysian Peninsula in South-East Asia. Due to its strategic location, Singapore has benefitted greatly as an international business and communications center. It is, however, not endowed with any natural resources. As a result, the goal to make the nation a leader in Asia is dependent on:

  • Up-to-date technological advancements which can further promote international business efficiency,
  • Competitive academic and technological education and training for the only resource it does have--its people

This paper will attempt to examine in some detail the country's
  1. Vision to be a leader in Information Technology
  2. Information Technology in Education
  3. The Interconnected Nation and
  4. Future of Information Technology

    A Leader in Information Technology

    Today, Singapore has become a thriving dynamic center for commerce and industry -- its economy has been ranked the least risky among Asian countries after Japan. The Singapore dollar has been growing strong every year, and its port is still the busiest in the world. Singapore has also been placed among top nations in the world in terms of strategic exploitation of Information Technology by companies, computer literacy of workers and telecommunications infrastructure.

    The government's next step is to make sure it stays competitive in this Information Age. Therefore, an efficient, global system of communication and information transfer is essential for maintaining it's current position as an international trading center -- businesses will definitely do better if they are linked to their business partners, other related businesses, the government, and other relevant information resources. Advanced information technology will help them to attain information which would otherwise be unaccessible or slower to retrieve.

    On August 1991, after conducting a rigorous study of the major economic sectors of Singapore, and after consulting with over 200 senior executives from both the public and private business sectors, the IT2000 Vision was formulated. This vision for "the Next Lap" would bring about an advanced "Nationwide Information Infrastructure" (NII) which will interlink computers in every home, office, school and factory. Computers will link Singaporeans to the rest of the world, and will therefore evolve into an "information appliance" in the same way the telephone and television did, enabling its users to access electronically stored information to improve business. The NII Framework will be built using the existing network structure, and a smaller experimental infrastructure is designed to moderate and control the proceeding of the construction.

    Like the U.S. NII, the applications of the information infrastructure are believed to be able to benefit many. Both plans are similar -- the U.S. NII is committed to using high-performance computing and high-speed networking to expand and speed up information access and needs to be administered through multi-agency collaboration. Singapore's IT2000 Vision similarly focuses on putting a national network through bridging, enhancing and expanding its existing infrastructure.

    Information Technology in Education

    Singapore's Ministry of Education aims to nurture talent and develop individual potential to the fullest. It has the important yet difficult task of making sure the younger generation do not loose their cultural roots while training them to keep up with the latest innovations and technological advances. Today, Singapore has one of the most technologically advanced education systems in Asia -- advanced information technology is available in many schools. The National University of Singapore itself functions as a WWW server in Singapore. WWW sites are found in almost all Singapore Junior Colleges (equivalent to highschools in the U.S.), and technological education and training is a high priority in the academic agenda.

    With the recent IT2000 Vision, a national project devoted to education in the Information Age is launched. The Student's and Teacher's Workbench (STW) is a joint project between the Ministry of Education (MOE) and the National Computer Board (NCB), which was largely responsible for the initiation of the IT2000 masterplan. As its name suggests, the STW has 2 major components: The Student's Workbench and the Teacher's Workbench. It provides access to Internet and other text, image, audio, and video lesson materials. Retrieval, layout, tagging, annotation as well as browsing of media items will also be supported.

    With the use of Information Technology, students can have access to a large variety of learning and educational materials from all over the world. They can also establish world-wide communications with people from different countries. And most importantly, they will learn the key to effective utilization of information technology at a very young age. In the long-run, this knowledge will benefit the country's growth and prosperity.

    A National Multi-Agency Collaboration

    The collaboration between the major WWW servers in Singapore are both planned and intended. They include the Memex Research PTE LTD, the Technet Unit, the SunSITE Singapre, and the Singapore Telecom. The Memex Research takes its name and inspiration from Vannevar Bush, a pioneer in computer design. Bush coined the term "Memex" in an article As We May Think. There, the term is defined as "a future device for individual use, which is a sort of mechanized private file and library." Memex's mission is to deliver enterprise-wide solution for managing resources effectively and to prepare for emerging technology. The Technet is a global communication technology with full Internet access. It is also the premier organization in Singapore in charge of the promotion of the Internet's widespread usage. The SunSITE Singapore operates out of the National University of Singapore's Computer Center. It is a SPARCserver 1000 with 4 CPUs and 6GB of disk space. Finally, the Singapore Telecom is licensed by the Telecommunication Authority of Singapore to provide telecommunication services.

    Memex, Technet, and SunSITE work closely together to provide services to all public and private sectors of the country. For example, the Memex service is maintained through a collaborative project between the Memex Research Pte Ltd and the Technet Unit and is hosted at SunSITE. The Technet Unit is also managed and operated by the Computer Center at the National University of Singapore. Singapore Telecom is somewhat different from the other 3 WWW servers because it is not a research or education institution. It's original and primary purpose is to provide telephone services to the public. However, as the Internet becomes more and more popular, Telecom started a new subsidiary division, the SingNet, which is a 24-hour service that gives users access to database linked to the Internet. It provides customer and product support to businesses over the Internet network and allows home users to e-mail world-wide.

    The Future of Information Technology in Singapore

    Singapore Telecom has been in the forefront of promoting and establishing the usage of telecommunications technology through a number of projects. On December 27, 1994, it announced it will lower rates for its international leased circuits by up to 25% starting January 1, 1995. On January 24, the company announced its plan to invest US$94 million in an ambitious United Kingdom project called I-CO, which will provide a seamless, global mobile service to its customers via a network of 12 satellites, providing different telecommunication services. Then on January 25, 1995, the company and three other major telecommunications carriers in South-East Asia announced their plan to coordinate the telecommunications needs of businesses in the region. "Telecommunications infrastructure plays a vital role in the region's economic development...our initial services will include intra-regional VSAT and private leased services and facilities management." said the company spokesman.

    With this expansion of the nation's telecommunication service to the rest of the world and the company's belief in the importance of Information Technology, it is very likely Singapore will continue to preserve its position as a leader in international business and trade. Telecom's vision to be a global company and investor will provide the infrastructure needed for it to become an efficient global information center in the future.

    At home, a challenge was given to more than 135 principals, teachers and MOE headquarter officers at a talk on Using Internet on November 5th, 1994. Fascinating information about the history of the Internet, popular services available via the Internet, and application of Internet in education was given, and every principal, teacher and officer was encouraged to incorporate the use of Internet into the school curriculum. The promotion of Information Technology in education is aimed not only to improve the quality of education itself, but also to educate and familiarize the younger generation with advanced Information Technology. SunSITE, Technet and Memex, with its strong focus on research and education, will be able to support the nation's goal in education. Also, since the major Internet service is hosted by SunSITE at the University of Singapore, the plan to push the technology in and through education has already begun. Such an ideal collaboration between the major players of Information Technology, along with the unceasing support of the government, is sure to produce positive results.

    To some, this collaboration between the government and government-affiliated agencies strongly suggests a "monopoly" of the Information Technology service because it gives them too much control over the entire service offering. It is therefore important that laws regulating the use and access of electronic information by home users, businesses AND the government are well thought-out and planned to prevend the abuse of such an efficient and powerful technology.
