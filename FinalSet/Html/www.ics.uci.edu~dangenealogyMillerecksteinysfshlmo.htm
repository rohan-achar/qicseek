I came across your very fine genealogy and I believe that we have a common

My name is Barbara Jean Greil (nee Eckstein)and my great-grandfather was the
Yosef Shlomo Eckstein mentioned in your genealogy.  What you may not know is
that Chava Rosner was Yosef Shlomo's 2nd wife.  He was briefly married to
another woman, possibly by the name of Lya? and they were divorced.  They
did have a son, my grandfather, (Zvi Aliemelech ben Josef Shlomo known as
Herman) who lived in Cluj Romania (was Hungary before WWI and called
Kolozsvar or Klausenberg.)  Herman married Emma Levy, came to the U.S. and
settled in Pittsburgh.  Emma came with their 7 children in 1926 and followed
him to Pgh.  My father, Alex Eckstein is one of those children.

I can provide you with alot of information about this part of the family and
quite a bit about the Rosner side as well.  I also have a second cousin in
the NYC area (Brooklyn, I believe) who has been working extensively on the
Eckstein/Rosner genealogy.  I just heard from him recently, which sparked my
interest in doing some research on the internet, which wasn't a factor when
we last corresponded in the 1980's!  With his permission, I will put you in
touch with him.

I am really impressed by your work! I have found alot of Eckstein
genealogies on the Internet, but none of them are Jewish.  Of course I am
just a "newbie" in all this.

Let me know if you would like me to give you more information.  I live in
upstate NY in the Village of Alfred.  My husband is a sociology professor at
Alfred University, and I am a librarian at the SUNY College of Technology at
Alfred.  My brother lives in New City, and my Aunt Irene (Eckstein) Kohn
lives on the Lower East Side, so I do get down to the city fairly often.

Hi there everyone-
 
Recently I have been in touch with a second cousin of ours, Avery
Horowitz, who my parents met many years ago in Brooklyn.  Over the years
he has been compiling an Eckstein genealogy and he recently got in touch
with me.  He would like to update his records about our part of the
family--births, marriages, deaths etc.  Any information you could provide
or corroborate would be greatly appreciated.

He would like the Hebrew and English names of your parents, of you and
your siblings, of your children, and their children if there are any.
Your birthplace and that of your parents and children, grandchildren,
would also be great.  Marriage and death dates are also important.
Anything you can provide would be terrific.

After hearing from Avery, I decided to look on the internet to see what
else I could find out about Ecksteins.  Lots of non-Jewish Ecksteins, but
I <did> find an extensive genealogy to the Langsam, Miller, Spria,
Eckstein genealogy.  It seems as though we are related to these families.
I have been in touch with Moishe Miller, who is compiling this genealogy.
He has done a wonderful job, as has Avery.  Here is a link to Moishe's
work:  http://www.ics.uci.edu/~dan/genealogy/Miller/langsam.htm
 
You may be wondering how we are related to Avery Horowitz.  I hope I get
this right!  His mother is Helen Applebaum Horowitz, and she is the
daughter of Goldie Eckstein Applebaum.  Goldie was one of the nine
children of our great-grandfather Yosef Shlomo Eckstein and Chava Rosner
(his second wife, according to Avery). Remember that Yosef Shlomo and our
great-grandmother Lya or Lia Sternberger or Steinberger had one child,
Herman, our grandfather, and then they divorced.  Therefore Avery and all
of us share the same great-great grandfather.  (A side note:  my Dad
believes that his grandfather's second wife was someone named Friedman,
because he remembers having cousins with the last name of Rosner.
According to Avery, this is because Lya, the first wife, married a cousin
of Chava's named Morton Rosner.  Just to make it  <really> interesting,
two of Lya and Morton Rosner's daughters married two Applebaum brothers;
this is in addition to the fact that Avery's grandmother Goldie also
married a man named Applebaum.  I am not sure if <these> Applebaums are
related)  If this seems confusing, believe me, it is!

I understand that Uncle Albert was also compiling information.  I am
hoping that we can all share this information.  Additional information
about grandfather Herman (his birthplace, date, marriage date(s), etc)
would be great.  Also, any information that anyone has on our great-great
grandfather Yosef Shlomo would be very important including birth/death
dates and places, his occupation.
 
If any of you have pictures of any of our ancestors it would be great to
create a digital archive.  I would be happy to scan any photographs and
post them to everyone.
 
Note:  I have a copy of the passenger manifest sheet from our parents voyage
on the SSRoosevelt in 1926.  If anyone wants a copy of this, I would be
happy to duplicate it and send it on.  (Carol, your copy will be in the mail
shortly.)


Hope you don't mind that I have contacted you this way, and I know this is
alot of work on your part.  Love and best wishes to all of you.
